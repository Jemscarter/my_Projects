Online Attack

This course dispensed by Tali Gazit from Bar Ilan University explores internet psychology focusing on a disturbing phenomenon: online attacks. We examine various forms of violence manifesting online, their causes and consequences, while trying to find ways to address this problem.

An Image about online Violence

Image : Factors Contributing to Online Violence

The image shows a woman wearing a mask behind her computer, with Hebrew text asking: What factors enable online violence?

The answer highlights three key points:

  1. The attacker feels protected online
  2. In most cases, the victim cannot respond to the attacker
  3. Lack of enforcement

These factors create what psychologists call the online disinhibition effect - where anonymity and physical distance make people more likely to engage in hostile behavior.

For example, a person who would never insult someone face-to-face might leave cruel comments on social media, or someone might share private information knowing they're unlikely to face consequences.

Table illustrating the different types of online attacks and their target groups :

Types of Online Attacks
Type of Aggression Description Characteristics Target Group
Cyberbullying Verbal and emotional violence through digital platforms. Use of humiliation, intimidation, spreading false rumors, harassment, threats, and isolation. Children and teenagers, but also adults.
Online Harassment Disturbing and harmful behavior against a person online. Includes harassing messages, online stalking, spreading personal information, and sexual harassment. Anyone, but especially women and minorities.
Cyber Terrorism Using the internet to promote terrorist actions and recruitment. Member recruitment, communication, propaganda distribution, fundraising, spreading intimidating messages. Terrorist groups exploiting online platforms.
Online Sexism Stereotypical treatment of women, portraying them as inferior or sexual objects. Spreading gender stereotypes, sexual harassment, offensive comments about appearance and sexuality, sharing intimate images without consent. Women and girls.
Hate Speech Offensive and racist expressions directed at specific groups. Use of offensive language, humiliation, racism, and discrimination based on religion, race, gender, or sexual orientation. Minorities, marginalized groups, and any group suffering from discrimination.
Doxxing Publishing personal information online without consent to harm someone. Exposing names, addresses, phone numbers, and other sensitive personal information. Anyone, especially people who expressed different views.
Online Account Hijacking Breaking into and taking control of social media accounts with intent to harm. Taking over social media accounts, changing passwords, accessing personal information, posting harmful content. Anyone, especially public figures like media personalities and politicians.
Online Shaming Publishing harmful information or images on social media to humiliate the victim. Public exposure of negative information about a person, using digital platforms to humiliate the victim. Anyone, but especially people with public exposure.
